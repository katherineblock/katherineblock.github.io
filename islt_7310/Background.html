Background

Courses:

Fall 2018:

  • IS_LT 7301: Introduction to Information Technology (3 credits) This course covers the basics of information technology in the institutional setting, including standards, information culture, and networking.
  • IS_LT 7305: Foundations of Library and Information Science (3 credits) An introduction to information science, information professions, and the library as an information institution.

Spring 2019:

  • IS_LT 7314: Reference Sources and Services (3 credits) Discusses the basics of reference sources and the practice of reference services. Includes predicting trends in the future of reference in libraries.
  • IS_LT 9410: Seminar in Information Science and Learning Technology (3 credits) This course stimulates discussion on sociocultural and social constructivist theory as they relate to literacy.
  • IS_LT 9434: Teen Library Materials (3 credits) This course covers the characteristics of materials for adolescents, adolescent interests, and how to analyze adolescent material. Students will read and analyze various adolescent novels throughout the course.

Summer 2019:

  • IS_LT 9012: Public Library Community Partnerships (3 credits) Through this course students are given the opportunity to develop a partnership with a local community organization. This course also covers the basics of a successful partnership and how to use specific leadership skills.

Fall 2019:

  • IS_LT 7315: Management of Information Agencies (3 credits) Covers the content of management as it applies to libraries and information professions. Includes theory, models, and applied practice of management.
  • IS_LT 9431: Children’s Library Materials (3 credits) This course covers materials for children, analysis of children’s materials, and how to plan programming for children.
  • IS_LT 9433: Youth Services in Libraries (3 credits) This course discusses children and teenage use of the library. It discusses child and adolescent development and the role the library plays in this development. Students create a community analysis and learn how to use the analysis to create beneficial programming for the community.

Spring 2020:

  • IS_LT 9085: Problems in Information Science and Learning Technology (3 credits) This course was taken as a research independent study partnered with a professor at the University of Missouri. Research was focused on the capacity of rural school libraries as anchors for community mental health efforts.
  • IS_LT 7312: Principles of Cataloging and Classification (3 credits) Covers the theory and practice of cataloging and classification in libraries.
  • IS_LT 7313: Collection and Access Management (3 credits) This course covers the selection of materials for libraries or other information agencies, policies surrounding collection management, and intellectual freedom.
  • IS_LT 7310: Seminar in Information Science and Learning Technology (3 credits) This course shows students how to design and develop websites using HTML and CSS3. Students finish this course with a full functional website that they designed.
  • IS_LT 9410: Seminar in Information Science and Learning Technology (1 credit) This course is designed to help graduating students complete their ePortfolio to its fullest extent.
